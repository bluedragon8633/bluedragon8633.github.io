Acids, Bases, & Salts

Acid properties

  • Produce H+ in aqueous solutions, which combines with water to make HO+
  • pH below 7; turn litmus paper red
  • Polar covalent compounds; never contain metals

Naming of Acids

Generic formula: HA(aq)

H H+ ion(s)
A Anion

Classes of acids

Binary: H + single element anion (ex. HF, HCl)

Ternary: H + polyatomic anion

Ending Acid name
-ide hydro-(stem)-ic acid
-ate (stem)-ic acid
-ite (stem)-ous acid

REMEMBER:

  • OH and CN end with -ide
  • Sulfur-, not sulf-
  • Phosphor-, not phosph-

Base Properties

  • React with skin fats/oils to produce soap- feel slippery/slimy to the touch
  • pH greater than 7; turn litmus paper blue
  • Produce OH- ions in aqueous solution (directly or indirectly)
    • Directly: NaOH --> OH- + Na+
    • Indirectly: NH3 + H2O --> NH4+ + OH-
