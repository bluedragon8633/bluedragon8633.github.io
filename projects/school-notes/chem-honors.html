Properties

  • Physical Properties: measurable/observable with 5 senses
    • Includes dissolving, phase change
    • Usually reversible (ex. phase change); not always though (ex. lacerations)
    • Extensive physical properties: dependent on amount (ex. calories, mass, volume)
    • Intensive physical properties: dependent on type (ex. hardness, density, melting point)
  • Chemical Properties
    • Describe behavior/potential to undergo chemical change when exposed to other substances/energy
    • Usually irreversible
    • Only observable during chemical reactions
    • 5 Signs of Chemical Change
      • Emission of gas (ex. bubbling, smoke, cloud)
      • Color changes
      • Temperature changes (exothermic releases heat, endothermic absorbs)
      • Formation of precipitants-insoluble solides made from 2 aqueous solutions
      • Emission of light (flash, flame)

Metals vs. Nonmetals

  • Metals

    • Hard, shiny, conductors of electricity and heat
    • Malleable and ductile
    • Mostly in groups 1-12

  • Nonmetals

    • Soft, brittle, dull as solids
    • All found in groups 13-18 (and Hydrogen)
    • Gain electrons to become anions

  • Metalloids

Ions

  • Cations
    • Positive charge
    • Made most commonly by metals
    • Ionic radius is smaller than OG- fewer electrons means they don't have to spread as much
  • Anions
    • Negative charge
    • Made by nonmetals
    • Larger atomic radius because more electrons repel each other more

Bonding

  • Ionic Bonding
    • Between metal and nonmetal
    • Complete transfer of electrons to fill valence shells
  • Covalent Bonding
    • Between nonmetals only
    • Sharing of electrons-valence shells overlap
